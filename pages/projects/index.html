My projects

This page contains some lists of things I've worked on over the years in my spare time.

Some projects have additional information, which can be found by clicking on their names. Source repositories are typically also provided for public viewing or contributions.

Note that the dates provided for each entry are not strict and in some cases the 'end' date is just a guess as to when I last worked on it, as opposed to a confirmation of the project actually being complete.

Further documentation

I have recently reserved docs.kosude.net to serve as a central location for each project's documentation, but this is currently a work-in-progress. A lot of my projects are already somewhat documented, and this is currently accessible through their upstream repositories.

List of personal electronics projects

  • embedded systems

    • The AmBR Box

      Source

      May 2026 - current

      •

      6502, assembly

      A lightweight retro-style games console project based on the W65C816 chip (an advancement on the original Mos 6502), implementing a programmable computer architecture from the ground up. Likely my most involved project yet: AmBR ("amber") stands for Ambitious But Rubbish.

    • DOOMbox

      Source

      May 2025 - current

      •

      digital, rp2350, c, assembly

      An RP2350-based system dedicated to running a purpose-built DOOM clone. This is an evolution and hardware-based equivalent of my x86 DOOM kernel, which is listed in another entry below.
